Defense Readiness Check

A planning tool that runs your situation against the Graduate School calendar, the NOTIF eight-week rule, and the 767 enrollment requirement, and tells you whether the term you want is realistic.

This tool's calendar data ends August 2027. The hard-coded term deadlines no longer cover today's date. Verify all dates directly with the UK Graduate School and your DGS before acting.

What this tool is

A planning aid for the procedural side of defense timing: NOTIF, calendar deadlines, milestones, enrollment, and how your target term maps onto real dates.

What it isn't

A substitute for your chair, committee, or DGS. It can't read your draft, judge defense readiness, or override Graduate School guidance.

Faculty are off-contract May 15 – August 15. If your plan depends on summer faculty work, your plan needs to change.

Always confirm with your chair, your DGS, and the Graduate School before acting.

Have you completed two semesters of post-qualifying residency (767)?
You must complete two semesters of 767 after passing your qualifying exam before you can sit for the final defense.
Is a complete draft of the dissertation in your committee chair's hands — and has your chair reviewed and approved it for distribution?
The formal NOTIF rule: chair must have a complete draft that they have reviewed and approved before NOTIF can be filed. "Sent but not reviewed" does not count.
Have all your committee members had the chance to review at least some part of your dissertation?
The EDL Dissertation Defense page asks the student to have every committee member review at least some part before NOTIF — and you verify this on the form itself.
For your target term, will you be enrolled in 767 (or 2hr post-residency)?
During the academic year (fall and spring), Graduate School policy is clear: you cannot defend without 767 (or 2hr post-residency) enrollment in that term. Summer is exempt if you are scheduled to defend.
